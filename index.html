Regular concrete is poured and finished plain — either broom-textured or smooth. Stamped concrete uses textured rubber mats pressed into the wet concrete to create patterns that mimic stone, slate, brick, or wood. Color pigments are added before or after pouring. Stamped concrete costs 20–40% more than plain but dramatically elevates the look of patios, pool decks, and walkways.

In East Texas, spring (March–May) and fall (September–November) are the ideal seasons for pouring concrete.
